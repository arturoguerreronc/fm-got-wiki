The History of Westeros

A Timeline of Major Events

The history of Westeros spans thousands of years, from the mythical Age of Heroes to the War of the Five Kings and beyond. Below is a chronological account of the most significant events that shaped the Seven Kingdoms.

The Age of Heroes

~10,000 BC

A legendary period during which many of the noble houses of Westeros trace their founding. This era saw the rise of Bran the Builder, who constructed the Wall and founded House Stark, and Lann the Clever, who acquired Casterly Rock and founded House Lannister.

The Long Night

~8,000 BC

A winter that lasted a generation, during which the White Walkers descended upon Westeros from the far north, bringing with them cold and darkness. They were ultimately defeated in the Battle for the Dawn, after which the Wall was constructed to prevent their return.

Aegon's Conquest

1 AC

Aegon Targaryen, along with his sister-wives Visenya and Rhaenys, conquered six of the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros with their dragons. This marked the beginning of the Targaryen dynasty and the Seven Kingdoms as a unified realm.

The Dance of the Dragons

129-131 AC

A civil war within House Targaryen between two rival branches, resulting in the death of most of the Targaryen dragons. The conflict was fought between Rhaenyra Targaryen and her half-brother Aegon II over their father Viserys I's throne.

Robert's Rebellion

280-281 AC

Also known as the War of the Usurper, this rebellion led by Robert Baratheon, Ned Stark, and Jon Arryn overthrew the Targaryen dynasty after Prince Rhaegar allegedly abducted Lyanna Stark and King Aerys II executed Rickard and Brandon Stark.

The War of the Five Kings

298-300 AC

A major civil war that erupted after the death of King Robert Baratheon. Five men claimed the Iron Throne or independence from it: Joffrey Baratheon, Stannis Baratheon, Renly Baratheon, Robb Stark, and Balon Greyjoy.

The Great War

305 AC

The final conflict against the White Walkers and their army of the dead, culminating in the Battle of Winterfell. The living forces of Westeros, led by Jon Snow and Daenerys Targaryen, faced the Night King and his undead army.

Historical Artifacts

Valyrian Steel

A form of metal that was forged in the Freehold of Valyria. Exceptionally sharp and strong, these weapons are known to be capable of killing White Walkers.

Dragon Eggs

Petrified eggs from which dragons can hatch under specific circumstances. They are extremely rare and valuable after the extinction of dragons.

The Iron Throne

Forged from the swords of Aegon the Conqueror's defeated enemies, the Iron Throne serves as both the physical seat of the King of the Andals and the First Men, and as a symbol of power in the Seven Kingdoms.
